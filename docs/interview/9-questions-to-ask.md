# Questions to Ask After Your Interview

> Smart, thoughtful questions show genuine interest in the role and help **you** evaluate whether the company is the right fit. Never say "I have no questions."

---

## Questions for the Engineering Manager / Tech Lead

### About the Team & Role

1. **What does the team structure look like?** How many developers, QA, DevOps? What's the ratio of senior to junior?

2. **What would my first 30/60/90 days look like?** What would you expect me to accomplish in the first 3 months?

3. **What's the biggest technical challenge the team is facing right now?**

4. **Who would I be working with most closely?** Will I be collaborating directly with frontend, mobile, or data teams?

5. **What does a typical day or week look like for someone in this role?**

6. **How are tasks assigned?** Do developers pick from a backlog, or are tasks assigned by the lead?

---

### About Technology & Engineering Practices

7. **What's the tech stack?** (If not already discussed) What version of Java/Spring Boot? What databases, message brokers, cloud providers?

8. **How do you handle code reviews?** Is there a minimum number of approvals? What do you look for in reviews?

9. **What does your CI/CD pipeline look like?** How often do you deploy to production?

10. **How do you handle technical debt?** Is there dedicated time for refactoring, or is it done alongside feature work?

11. **Do you write tests?** What's the testing strategy — unit tests, integration tests, E2E? What's the approximate code coverage?

12. **What development methodology do you follow?** Scrum, Kanban, or something else? Sprint length?

13. **How do you handle incidents in production?** Is there an on-call rotation? What's the escalation process?

14. **Are there opportunities to learn new technologies or experiment?** Do you allocate time for R&D or hackathons?

---

### About Growth & Culture

15. **How do you measure developer performance?** Is it based on output, impact, peer reviews, or OKRs?

16. **What does career growth look like here?** Is there a clear path from mid-level → senior → staff/principal engineer?

17. **How does the team handle disagreements on technical decisions?**

18. **What's the best thing about working on this team?** What keeps people here?

19. **What's one thing you'd like to improve about the team or process?**

20. **Is there a mentorship culture?** Will I have a buddy or mentor during onboarding?

---

## Questions for HR / People Team

### About Compensation & Benefits

21. **What is the salary range for this position?** (If not discussed yet) Is there room for negotiation based on experience?

22. **What benefits does the company offer?** Health insurance, dental, life insurance? Does it cover family members?

23. **Is there a bonus or performance-based incentive?** How often are performance reviews and salary adjustments?

24. **Are there stock options or equity?** What's the vesting schedule?

25. **How many paid leave days per year?** Sick leave, personal leave, public holidays?

26. **Is there a probation period?** How long, and what are the evaluation criteria?

---

### About Work Environment & Policies

27. **What's the work arrangement?** Fully on-site, hybrid, or remote? How many days in-office?

28. **What are the working hours?** Is there flexibility in start/end times? Core hours?

29. **Is there an overtime policy?** How is overtime compensated — paid, time off, or expected as part of the role?

30. **What tools does the company provide?** Laptop, monitor, software licenses? Can I choose Mac or Windows?

31. **Is there a budget for learning and development?** Conference attendance, online courses (Udemy, Pluralsight), certifications?

32. **What's the dress code?** Casual, business casual, or formal?

---

### About Company & Culture

33. **How would you describe the company culture?** What are the core values?

34. **What's the team turnover rate?** How long do developers typically stay?

35. **How does the company support work-life balance?**

36. **Are there team activities or social events?** Team outings, team building, company trips?

37. **What's the company's financial health?** Is the company profitable, funded, or bootstrapped? Any recent funding rounds?

38. **What's the company's growth plan for the next 1-2 years?** Any new products, markets, or hiring plans?

---

### About the Hiring Process

39. **What are the next steps in the interview process?** How many more rounds? Timeline?

40. **When can I expect to hear back?** What's the typical time from final interview to offer?

41. **Is there anything about my background or experience that concerns you?** *(Shows confidence and gives you a chance to address doubts)*

42. **Is there anything else I can provide to help with the decision?** References, portfolio, code samples?

## Red Flags to Watch For 🚩

Pay attention to these warning signs during the interview:

| Red Flag | What It Might Mean |
|----------|-------------------|
| "We're like a family here" | Blurred boundaries, expected overtime |
| No clear answer on tech stack or process | Disorganized team, no standards |
| "We wear many hats" | Undefined roles, too many responsibilities |
| High turnover or vague answers about retention | People leave frequently for a reason |
| No code reviews or testing | Low code quality, risky deployments |
| "Fast-paced environment" (overemphasized) | Chronic overwork, unrealistic deadlines |
| Can't describe career growth path | No promotion opportunities |
| Interviewer speaks negatively about current team | Toxic culture |
| No mention of onboarding plan | Sink-or-swim culture |
| Salary discussion is avoided entirely | Below-market compensation |

## Tips for Asking Questions

- **Prepare 5-8 questions** — you won't have time for all of them, so prioritize.
- **Don't ask things you can easily Google** — company founding year, office location, etc.
- **Tailor to the interviewer** — ask technical questions to the manager, benefits questions to HR.
- **Take notes** — write down answers so you can compare offers later.
- **Ask about specifics, not generics** — "What testing framework do you use?" is better than "Do you write tests?"
- **It's OK to ask about salary and benefits** — this is a professional negotiation, not a favor.
- **Save your strongest question for last** — it's what they'll remember.

---

## Power Questions — Leave a Strong Impression

These questions show strategic thinking and genuine interest:

> **"If I were to join, what would success look like in this role after 6 months?"**

> **"What's the most impactful project the team shipped recently, and what made it successful?"**

> **"How does engineering influence product decisions here? Do developers have input on what to build?"**

> **"What's the one thing that would make you confident that hiring me was the right decision?"**

> **"Is there anything about my profile that you'd like me to clarify or elaborate on?"**
