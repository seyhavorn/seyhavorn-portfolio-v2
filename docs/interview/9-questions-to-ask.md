# Questions to Ask After Your Banking Interview

> Smart, thoughtful questions show genuine interest in the role and help **you** evaluate whether the bank is the right fit. Never say "I have no questions."

---

## Questions for the Engineering Manager / Tech Lead

### About the Team & Role

1. **What does the team structure look like?** How many backend developers? Is there a dedicated DevOps/SRE team, or do developers handle infrastructure?

2. **What would my first 30/60/90 days look like?** What would you expect me to accomplish in the first 3 months? Is there an onboarding program for new engineers?

3. **What's the biggest technical challenge the team is facing right now?** Are you migrating from a legacy system? Building new digital banking features?

4. **Who would I be working with most closely?** Will I collaborate directly with the core banking team, mobile team, or compliance team?

5. **What does a typical day or week look like for someone in this role?** What's the ratio of feature development vs maintenance vs incident response?

6. **How are tasks assigned?** Scrum-based sprint planning, or more ad-hoc? Who prioritizes — engineering or product/business?

---

### About Technology & Engineering Practices

7. **What's the tech stack?** What version of Java/Spring Boot? What databases? What message brokers? Cloud or on-premise?

8. **What core banking system do you use?** Temenos T24, Finastra, custom-built, or something else? How does the backend integrate with it?

9. **How do you handle code reviews?** Is there a minimum number of approvals? What do you look for in reviews? Is there a formal change management process?

10. **What does your CI/CD pipeline look like?** How often do you deploy to production? Is there a staging environment that mirrors production?

11. **How do you handle technical debt?** Is there dedicated time for refactoring, or is it done alongside feature work?

12. **Do you write tests?** What's the testing strategy — unit tests, integration tests, E2E? What's the approximate code coverage? Do you use Testcontainers?

13. **What development methodology do you follow?** Scrum, Kanban, or something else? Sprint length? How are releases managed?

14. **How do you handle production incidents?** Is there an on-call rotation? What's the escalation process? What's the average incident resolution time?

15. **How do you manage database schema changes?** Flyway, Liquibase? Do you have a zero-downtime migration strategy?

16. **What's your approach to API design?** OpenAPI/Swagger? Versioning strategy? Do you follow any specific standards (ISO 20022, Open Banking)?

---

### About Security & Compliance

17. **What security practices does the engineering team follow?** Code security scanning, dependency vulnerability scanning, penetration testing?

18. **Are you PCI DSS compliant?** How does that impact the development workflow?

19. **How do you handle sensitive data in development?** Do developers have access to production data? Is there data masking?

20. **What's the change management process for production deployments?** Is there a Change Advisory Board (CAB)? How long does approval take?

---

### About Growth & Culture

21. **How do you measure developer performance?** Is it based on output, impact, peer reviews, or OKRs?

22. **What does career growth look like here?** Is there a clear path from senior → lead → principal engineer or architect?

23. **How does the team handle disagreements on technical decisions?**

24. **What's the best thing about working on this team?** What keeps people here?

25. **What's one thing you'd like to improve about the team or process?**

26. **Is there a mentorship culture?** Will I have a buddy or mentor during onboarding?

27. **Are there opportunities to attend conferences or get certifications?** Does the bank support AWS/GCP certifications or Spring certification?

---

## Questions for HR / People Team

### About Compensation & Benefits

28. **What is the salary range for this position?** Is there room for negotiation based on experience?

29. **What benefits does the company offer?** Health insurance, dental, life insurance? Does it cover family members?

30. **Is there a bonus or performance-based incentive?** How often are performance reviews and salary adjustments? Is there a 13th month salary?

31. **Are there stock options or profit-sharing?** (For fintech companies)

32. **Do bank employees get preferential loan rates or banking benefits?** (Common perk at banks)

33. **How many paid leave days per year?** Sick leave, personal leave, public holidays?

34. **Is there a probation period?** How long, and what are the evaluation criteria?

---

### About Work Environment & Policies

35. **What's the work arrangement?** Fully on-site, hybrid, or remote? How many days in-office?

36. **What are the working hours?** Is there flexibility in start/end times? Core hours?

37. **Is there an on-call rotation?** How is on-call compensated — paid, time off, or expected as part of the role?

38. **What tools does the company provide?** Laptop, monitor, software licenses? Can I choose Mac or Windows?

39. **Is there a budget for learning and development?** Conference attendance, online courses, certifications?

40. **What's the dress code?** Business formal, business casual, or casual?

---

### About Company & Culture

41. **How would you describe the company culture?** What are the core values?

42. **What's the team turnover rate?** How long do developers typically stay?

43. **How does the company support work-life balance?**

44. **Are there team activities or social events?** Team outings, team building?

45. **What's the company's financial health?** (For fintech startups)

46. **What's the company's technology roadmap for the next 1-2 years?** Digital banking expansion? New payment channels? Open Banking APIs?

---

### About the Hiring Process

47. **What are the next steps in the interview process?** How many more rounds? Timeline?

48. **When can I expect to hear back?** What's the typical time from final interview to offer?

49. **Is there a background check or security clearance process?** (Standard for banks)

50. **Is there anything about my background or experience that concerns you?** *(Shows confidence and gives you a chance to address doubts)*

51. **Is there anything else I can provide to help with the decision?** References, portfolio, code samples?

## Red Flags to Watch For 🚩

Pay attention to these warning signs during the interview:

| Red Flag | What It Might Mean |
|----------|-------------------|
| "We're like a family here" | Blurred boundaries, expected overtime |
| No clear answer on tech stack or process | Disorganized team, no standards |
| "We wear many hats" | Undefined roles, too many responsibilities |
| High turnover or vague answers about retention | People leave frequently for a reason |
| No code reviews or testing | Low code quality, risky deployments |
| "Fast-paced environment" (overemphasized) | Chronic overwork, unrealistic deadlines |
| No mention of compliance or security practices | Immature engineering culture for a bank |
| Developers have direct production DB access with no audit | Major security red flag |
| No staging environment | Changes go directly to production — risky |
| "We'll figure it out as we go" | No architecture, no planning |
| Can't describe career growth path | No promotion opportunities |
| Salary discussion is avoided entirely | Below-market compensation |

## Banking-Specific Green Flags ✅

| Green Flag | What It Means |
|------------|--------------|
| Clear change management process | Mature engineering operations |
| Dedicated DevOps/SRE team | Infrastructure is taken seriously |
| Automated testing in CI/CD | Code quality is valued |
| Regular security audits | Compliance-aware culture |
| Structured onboarding program | They invest in new hires |
| Technology roadmap exists | Engineering has strategic direction |
| Code review requirements | Quality control is in place |
| Mentorship culture | Senior engineers willing to teach |

## Tips for Asking Questions

- **Prepare 5-8 questions** — you won't have time for all of them, so prioritize.
- **Don't ask things you can easily Google** — company founding year, number of branches, etc.
- **Tailor to the interviewer** — ask technical questions to the manager, benefits questions to HR.
- **Take notes** — write down answers so you can compare offers later.
- **Ask about specifics, not generics** — "What testing framework do you use?" is better than "Do you write tests?"
- **It's OK to ask about salary and benefits** — this is a professional negotiation, not a favor.
- **Save your strongest question for last** — it's what they'll remember.
- **Banking-specific:** Ask about compliance and change management — it shows you understand banking is different from startups.

## Power Questions — Leave a Strong Impression

These questions show strategic thinking and genuine interest:

> **"If I were to join, what would success look like in this role after 6 months?"**

> **"What's the most impactful project the team shipped recently, and what made it successful?"**

> **"How does engineering influence product decisions here? Do developers have input on what banking features to build?"**

> **"What's the biggest risk to the current system's reliability, and what's the plan to address it?"**

> **"How do you balance speed of delivery with compliance requirements? What does your change approval process look like?"**

> **"Is there anything about my profile that you'd like me to clarify or elaborate on?"**
